Hello! This is my first post, and I'll use it to give some insight into who I am.

So, as you probably know by now, my name is Jack Miller. As of me writing this I am 19 years old and about to be finishing up my second semester of college at the University of Central Florida. Here, I am pursuing a bachelor's degree in information technology, though this wasn't always my plan.

I spent pretty much the entirety of high school preparing myself to go into aerospace engineering, as I have always loved aviation. This all changed in my senior year when I took AP Calculus BC (and hated it). I realized that these high-level math classes weren't for me. I was able to get by with decent grades, but I thought it was boring. Just the thought of going to that class each day was mentally draining. I tbought to myself, "Do I really want to take any more calculus classes? Is all this math something I truly enjoy?" 

Spoiler alert, it wasn't, and in June 2023, before I even started my first day of college, I had changed majors to information technology. I believe it is important to make your career something you are truly passionate about. I have always had in interest in computers, but I dislike coding, so I could never be a software engineer or something of the sort. Cybersecurity (while it can still require a degree of coding, it is certainly more tolerable for me) seemed like the perfect field for me to get into.

Now, I don't regret my decision at all. I am enjoying learning about different areas of cybersecurity, and slowly determining which route I would like to take for my career.
